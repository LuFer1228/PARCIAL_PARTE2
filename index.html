CAÑO DULCE

Caño Dulce Beach is located on the northern coast of Colombia, near the city of Barranquilla in the Atlántico department. It's a small, relatively quiet beach known for its natural beauty and laid-back atmosphere, attracting both locals and tourists looking for a peaceful seaside escape. The beach offers a mix of soft sand and rocky areas, with warm waters from the Caribbean Sea.

1888

"1888 Puerto" refers to the newly opened Muelle 1888, an exciting development in Puerto Colombia. It's a gastronomic and cultural hub that brings together international cuisine from various regions, including Mediterranean, Asian, German, and more. It also offers visitors a chance to experience the history of Puerto Colombia, particularly its old maritime pier, which was once a major entry point for immigrants. MALECON

The Malecón of Barranquilla, officially known as Gran Malecón del Río, is a popular waterfront promenade along the Magdalena River. It stretches over several kilometers and serves as a recreational and cultural hub for both locals and tourists. The Malecón features parks, green spaces, restaurants, and event areas, making it a lively spot for walking, cycling, dining, and enjoying river views.
